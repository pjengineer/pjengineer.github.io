Intelligent Traffic Management
Vehicle navigation systems are used for guiding vehicles to their destination. These systems usually use GPS or inertial navigation systems or a combination of both for positioning the vehicle. The vehicle navigation systems use a computer, which determines the position of the vehicle , plans the route and gives the directions to the driver. The driver gives the location of his destination while starting his journey and the computer guides the driver by giving either audio or and visual instructions. The route the computer plans is usually optimized route; the route is the route optimized for distance or the route can be the most or the least used route.

Vehicle tracking systems are usually used for managing a fleet of vehicles. The vehicles of a fleet are fitted with GPS, which usually transmit the positional data of the vehicles to a central station. The central station is a monitoring station, where the position of vehicles is displayed on a GIS map. Vehicle tracking systems will be useful for the police and emergency response services. The central station usually diverts the vehicle nea rest to the site, where the vehicles are required. By using a wireless phone service or cellular phone network, real time corrections can be sent to the receivers fitted on the vehicles and better results can be obtained.

Intelligent Parking Assist System (IPAS)
Intelligent Parking Assist System (IPAS), also known as the Advanced Parking Guidance System (APGS) is a system which helps the driver to park the car automatically without any human intervention. The problem arises when there is traffic congestion and shortage of time.


Also, when the space is less or the driver needs to park accurately, this system comes in handy. This project reflects free parking space recognition method in order to overcome the common drawbacks of existing vision based target parking position designation methods in dark indoor parking site. We have tried to create the prototype of a car by building an autonomous robot with help of microcontroller and simple Dc motors. Thereafter the image of the arena is captured using a web-camera and processed using MATLAB to obtain the nearest vacant parking slot.
Then the bot goes and parks itself at the assigned parking slot. Basically, the prototype, on entering the arena will find an optimal place for itself to park and will act accordingly.

AUDIO EFFECTS USING TI DSP320C6713 DSK
In our project, we are implementing a Digital Signal Processor for audio effects which include reverberation, flanging, echo effect, pitch shifting and fading. The choice of a DSP for the same is justified because analog systems use discrete components and a particular system designed for one audio effect, say echo cannot be applied to other effects. In contrast, it is very easy to implement various effects in the DSP by simply changing the program and reconfiguring the DSP.

n this project, we have implemented some sample programs like LED effects, 1 KHz Sine Wave Generation in the C6713 using CCS to familiarize ourselves with the kit as well as coding. In the next part, we intend to implement the above audio effects using the same.
